# Jurassic English — Discovery Call Guide
**Internal use only · Jurassic English / World Wise Learning · March 2026**

---

## 1. Purpose

The Discovery Call is a no-cost, no-obligation 30-minute scoping conversation. It has two purposes:

**For the lead**: to understand what Jurassic English can do for their specific school, team, or organisation — not from a static document, but in dialogue with a person who can contextualise the programme to their situation.

**For WWL**: to gather the scope information required to issue a credible, appropriately sized proposal — and to confirm that the lead is a genuine fit before committing proposal time.

The call is not a pitch. It is a structured conversation with a defined output: a qualification decision and a clear next step.

---

## 2. Pre-Call Preparation Checklist

Complete this before every Discovery Call. All data points come from the §8 Structured Record in the intake email, plus any replies received since.

```
Contact context:
[ ] Full Name + Role — note seniority and decision authority
[ ] Organisation name — any prior knowledge? existing client?
[ ] Country / Region — confirm time zone; any regional context
[ ] Preferred contact method — Email / Phone / WhatsApp

CRM context:
[ ] Buyer Type — which question set to use (Section 4)
[ ] MAOI — primary service interest; frame the conversation around this
[ ] Alignment — Primary / Secondary / Context-Dependent — calibrate depth
[ ] Pricing Band — know the range; be ready to contextualise without quoting precisely
[ ] Org Size (if provided) — affects licence tier and group pricing
[ ] Timeline (if provided) — affects proposal urgency and approach
[ ] Message (§6) — read carefully; note any specific asks or flags

Pre-call checks:
[ ] Has the lead accessed the pricing briefing? (check tracker col. 15)
[ ] Is there a message or qualification flag in §6?
[ ] Are there any prior CRM notes from previous contact with this person/org?
[ ] Any known relationship with this school, institution, or network?
```

## 3. Recommended Call Agenda (30 Minutes)

| Time | Section | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| 0:00 – 2:00 | Welcome and framing | Introduce JE/WWL briefly; confirm the call is a no-obligation conversation to understand their context |
| 2:00 – 8:00 | Their situation (open) | Open-ended: what's their context, what prompted the enquiry, what are they hoping to achieve |
| 8:00 – 18:00 | Scoping questions | Buyer-type-specific questions (Section 4) |
| 18:00 – 24:00 | Service alignment | Briefly describe the matched service; sense-check the fit; address any questions from the briefing |
| 24:00 – 28:00 | Agree next step | Confirm what happens after this call; set a specific date or action |
| 28:00 – 30:00 | Close | Confirm follow-up action, timeline, and best contact |

**Call tone:**
- Lead with curiosity, not a pitch deck
- Use the lead's language about their school/org, not internal JE terminology
- Do not quote specific prices on the call unless the lead has already seen the access briefing — frame as a range subject to scoping
- If the conversation reveals a different primary MAOI than the form indicated, note it and adjust the question set mid-call
- If the lead asks about something outside the primary MAOI, note it; address it briefly; do not derail the scoping

## 4. Qualifying Questions by Buyer Type

Use the question set that matches the lead's `buyer_type` tag from §7 of the intake email. If the buyer type appears to have shifted since the form (e.g. an `other` lead is clearly a school administrator), pivot to the correct set.

---

### School Administrator / Academic Director (`school_admin`)

Primary MAOIs: School Licensing · Academic Consulting · Curriculum Review

**Core questions:**

1. "Can you tell me a bit about the school — age range, approximate number of students, and the curriculum framework you're working within?"
2. "What is your current English language provision, and what are you most looking to change or improve?"
3. "Are you thinking about a whole-school rollout, or a specific year group, department, or cohort?"
4. "Who else is involved in this decision — is this primarily your call, or does it involve a head of department, board, or finance team?"
5. "What is your rough timeline — are you planning for this academic year, or the next?"
6. "Have you considered the teacher-training dimension, or is this primarily about the curriculum structure and school licence?"
7. (If Curriculum Review is the MAOI): "Is there a specific framework or external assessment standard you need to align to — CEFR, IB, Cambridge, or a national curriculum?"

**What to listen for:**
- School size (determines licence tier — this is the primary scoping variable)
- Whether they are the decision-maker or need board/finance sign-off
- Whether teacher training is a parallel need (opens a second revenue line)
- Any previous curriculum review or external assessment history

**Offer at the end of this call:**
If school size and licence tier are clear: offer the no-cost Resource Allocation Audit before issuing the proposal.

---

### Teacher / CPD Lead (`teacher_cpd_lead`)

Primary MAOI: Teacher Training
Secondary: Curriculum Review · Digital Reasoning Engine

**Core questions:**

1. "Are you looking at this primarily for your own professional development, or as a CPD programme for your whole department or teaching staff?"
2. "How many teachers would be involved if you were to run a group training?"
3. "Is there an existing CPD framework or accreditation requirement this needs to align to — for example, iPGCE, PGCE, or an internal performance framework?"
4. "Are you thinking about a one-off training day, or building toward a sustained pathway — such as the Train-the-Trainer certification?"
5. "Does your school or institution already have a Jurassic English licence, or is this an independent CPD enquiry?"
6. "What is the process for approving CPD spend at your school — is this something you can approve directly?"

**What to listen for:**
- Individual vs. group training (affects pricing significantly)
- Whether a Train-the-Trainer pathway is the goal (higher value, longer engagement)
- Whether the school has an existing JE licence — if not, this may be worth a parallel `school_admin` conversation
- Decision authority for CPD budget approval

**Note:** If the school is not yet licensed, a school_admin referral running in parallel often yields a stronger outcome for both the teacher and the school.

---

### Parent / Guardian (`parent_guardian`)

Primary MAOI: Digital Reasoning Engine
Secondary: School Licensing · Curriculum Review

**The parent path is a routing conversation, not a commercial scoping session.**

**Core questions:**

1. "Is your child currently at a school that uses Jurassic English, or is this an independent enquiry?"
2. "What age and year group is your child in?"
3. "What is the primary goal — supplementary support at home, or are you exploring whether the school should adopt JE?"

**Routing after these questions:**

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Child's school is already licensed | Direct to school's JE coordinator |
| Child's school is not licensed | Offer school referral pathway; provide DRE standalone access info |
| Parent is actually a school decision-maker | Reclassify as `school_admin`; restart with school_admin question set |
| Parent wants to advocate to the school | Offer to connect with the school directly; provide school info pack |

**Do not:** issue a commercial proposal to a parent/guardian unless they are confirmed as a school administrator or institutional decision-maker.

---

### Institutional Partner (`institutional_partner`)

Primary MAOIs: Institutional Partnerships · Academic Consulting
Secondary: Teacher Training · Digital Reasoning Engine · School Licensing · Curriculum Review

**Core questions:**

1. "Can you tell me about your organisation — what you do, and what you see as the potential overlap with Jurassic English?"
2. "Are you thinking about distribution, co-delivery, licensing to your network, or something more bespoke?"
3. "What is the geographic scope you have in mind — regional, national, or international?"
4. "How many schools or institutions would be in your network, approximately?"
5. "Who would be the primary contact on your side for the scoping and agreement process?"
6. "Is there an existing timeline or business case driving this — for example, a funding cycle, grant period, or board decision?"
7. "Are there co-branding or joint QA requirements we should understand from the outset?"

**What to listen for:**
- Whether this is distribution (reseller/white-label) vs. co-delivery vs. network licensing — these have very different agreement structures
- Network size and geographic scope (bespoke pricing triggers at 1,500+ students or multi-country)
- Decision-making timeline and any external drivers (grants, funding deadlines, board cycles)
- Joint QA or accreditation expectations

**Note:** Partnership conversations typically require a follow-up Partnership Scoping Consultation (a more detailed session, usually 60 minutes). Agree this at the end of the Discovery Call.

---

### Other (`other`)

Primary MAOIs: Teacher Training · Curriculum Review · Academic Consulting
(treated as Context-Dependent until reclassified)

**The `other` path is a reclassification conversation first.**

**Step 1 — Reclassify (first 5–8 minutes):**

1. "Thanks for getting in touch. Before we get into the specifics, it would help to understand a bit more about your context. What is your role, and what does your organisation do?"
2. "What prompted you to look at Jurassic English specifically?"
3. "Who would be the end users of the programme — students, teachers, or both?"

**After the first three questions**, you should be able to assign a buyer type. Reclassify in the tracker and continue with the corresponding question set above.

If reclassification is unclear after three questions: ask directly — "Would you describe yourself more as a school administrator, a teacher looking at CPD, or someone working at an institutional or network level?"

**Do not** issue a proposal to a lead that remains `other` — resolve the reclassification first.

## 5. Fit Indicators

These signal a lead is a strong fit and should move efficiently through the pipeline.

### School Administrator
- School is a recognised international, bilingual, or EAL-focused institution
- Currently using or considering a CEFR, IB, or Cambridge-aligned programme
- Decision-maker is on the call or named and accessible
- Timeline is current or next academic year
- School size falls within a standard licensing tier (up to 1,500)
- Has reviewed the pricing briefing and not disqualified on cost
- Expresses interest in teacher training alongside the licence

### Teacher / CPD Lead
- Enquiring for a group of 8+ educators (group rate applies)
- CPD programme is formally tracked or needs external accreditation alignment
- School is either already licensed or the teacher has influence with school leadership
- Interest in the Train-the-Trainer pathway
- Has a specific implementation timeline (term start date or CPD day)

### Institutional Partner
- Clearly articulated network or distribution model
- Decision-maker is present or named
- Network includes 5+ schools or 500+ students
- Has an existing curriculum partnership or QA framework to align with
- Has a specific timeline driven by an external factor (grant, board approval, academic year)

### Parent / Guardian (DRE path)
- Child is in a JE-licensed school and wants supplementary access
- Parent has already spoken to the school's JE coordinator
- Parent is willing to be directed to independent DRE access

## 6. Red Flags and Disqualification Indicators

These signal the need to pause, qualify further, or close the lead.

| Indicator | What to do |
|-----------|------------|
| Contact cannot name a specific school or organisation | Ask directly; if still vague after two attempts → `On Hold` |
| Lead is not the decision-maker and cannot identify who is | Move to `On Hold`; ask for an introduction to the decision-maker |
| Organisation is confirmed as a competitor | `Closed Lost` — log reason "Competitor enquiry" |
| Lead is seeking a trial or free pilot before any commitment | Note that JE does not offer free pilots; explain Discovery Call + Resource Allocation Audit as the no-cost entry point |
| Pricing range acknowledged as far beyond their budget | `Closed Lost` — log reason "Budget mismatch"; note if budget cycle may change this |
| Timeline is 2+ years out with no near-term driver | `On Hold` — set re-engagement reminder for 6–12 months out |
| Lead asks for confidential pricing to be shared with a third party or posted publicly | Decline; explain that access links are single-use per registered email |
| Lead is unresponsive after call — does not confirm any next step | `On Hold` — follow no-show protocol (two reschedule attempts; Day 10 → `On Hold`) |
| Org size is significantly misrepresented (stated 200 students, clearly 20) | Flag in Notes; clarify on the call; adjust pricing band before issuing proposal |
| Form message contains references to a grant or external funding that has already ended | Flag as Context-Dependent; qualify timeline before proceeding |

## 7. Post-Call Status Rules

Apply these rules immediately after the Discovery Call ends. Update the tracker before the end of the same business day.

| Call outcome | Status transition | Action required |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| All six qualification criteria met | `Awaiting Discovery Call` → `Qualified` | Write scope notes; set proposal target date; send brief follow-up email |
| Fit confirmed but one criterion outstanding (e.g. decision authority not confirmed) | Stay at `Awaiting Discovery Call` | Log the specific blocker; schedule a short follow-up or email to resolve it |
| Call held but scope is unclear — needs more information | Stay at `Awaiting Discovery Call` | Send a specific follow-up question by email; set a 5-day response window |
| Call held — lead is clearly not a fit | `Awaiting Discovery Call` → `Closed Lost` | Log reason; send a brief, professional close email |
| Lead no-show — first time | Stay at `Awaiting Discovery Call` | Send reschedule email Day of no-show |
| Lead no-show — second time | Stay at `Awaiting Discovery Call` | Send second reschedule offer Day 3 |
| Lead no-show — third time | → `On Hold` | Log "No-show x3"; set 30-day re-engagement check |
| Lead is `other` and reclassification reveals different BT | Update `buyer_type` in tracker; apply the correct status logic for the reclassified BT | Update CRM record |
| Lead is a parent routing to school referral | → `Closed Lost` (pipeline complete) or `On Hold` if school intro pending | Log outcome |

## 8. Recommended Next-Step Actions by Outcome

### Outcome: Qualified

Action sequence:
1. Send post-call follow-up email (same day or next morning) confirming the agreed next step
2. If Resource Allocation Audit is required: schedule it within 5 business days
3. Prepare proposal within 5 business days of qualification (or post-RAA if applicable)
4. Log Qualified date in tracker; set proposal target date in Notes

Post-call email subject: `Jurassic English — next steps after our conversation`

```
Hi [Name],

Great to speak with you today. Following our conversation, the next step is:

[One-sentence description of agreed action and date]

I'll [action] by [date]. Please reply to this email with any questions in the meantime.

Kind regards,
[Operator Name]
Jurassic English / World Wise Learning
info@jurassicenglish.com
```

---

### Outcome: Partially Qualified (one criterion outstanding)

Action sequence:
1. Send a focused follow-up email addressing the specific outstanding item
2. Set a 5-business-day response window
3. If resolved: move to `Qualified` and proceed
4. If not resolved within 5 days: move to `On Hold`; log reason and re-engagement date

---

### Outcome: Not a Fit

Action sequence:
1. Update status → `Closed Lost`; log reason
2. Send a brief, professional close email — no hard sell

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with us. Based on our conversation,
it sounds like the timing / fit isn't quite right at the moment.

If your situation changes, we would be glad to reconnect. In the meantime,
please don't hesitate to get in touch.

Kind regards,
[Operator Name]
Jurassic English / World Wise Learning
```

---

### Outcome: Reclassified Buyer Type

Action sequence:
1. Update `buyer_type` in tracker; note old BT → new BT in Notes column
2. Apply the correct pipeline path for the reclassified buyer type
3. If the call surfaced enough information to qualify: proceed directly to `Qualified`
4. If the reclassification changes the primary MAOI: update `maoi` in tracker and CRM

---

### Outcome: No-Show

Action sequence — three-attempt protocol:

| Attempt | Timing | Email content |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| 1st | Day of no-show | "Missed you today — happy to reschedule. Here are two options: [dates]" |
| 2nd | Day 3 | "Following up — would either of these times work? [dates]" |
| 3rd | Day 10 | "Leaving the door open if the timing works better later. Get in touch anytime." |
| After 3rd | Day 10+ | Status → `On Hold`; log "No-show x3"; set 30-day re-engagement check |

Do not move to `Closed Lost` after a no-show unless the lead explicitly declines.

## 9. Proposal-Readiness Criteria

A lead is ready for a proposal when all six of the following are confirmed. This is the qualification gate — do not issue a proposal without clearing all six.

```
[ ] 1. Org type and size confirmed
        (School size for licensing; group size for training; network size for partnerships)

[ ] 2. Primary MAOI / service confirmed and understood by the lead
        (They know what they are buying, not just that they are "interested in JE")

[ ] 3. Decision authority confirmed
        (Lead can approve, OR a named decision-maker has been identified and a
         clear path to them is established)

[ ] 4. Timeline is realistic
        (Current academic year or next — not 2+ years out without a specific trigger)

[ ] 5. Pricing range acknowledged
        (Lead has seen the access briefing or the range was discussed on the call,
         and they have not disqualified on cost)

[ ] 6. Agreed next step confirmed with a specific date
        (Not "let's stay in touch" — a named action and a date)
```

**If all six are yes**: status → `Qualified`; issue proposal within 5 business days.

**If one or more are no**: identify the specific blocker; log it; resolve it before moving to `Qualified`. Do not issue a proposal to an unqualified lead.
