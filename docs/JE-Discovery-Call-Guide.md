# Jurassic English — Discovery Call Guide
**Internal use only · Jurassic English / World Wise Learning · March 2026**

---

## 1. Purpose

The Discovery Call is a no-cost, no-obligation 30-minute conversation that serves two purposes:

1. **For the lead**: understand what JE can do for their specific school, team, or organisation — not from a static document, but in dialogue.
2. **For WWL**: gather the scope information needed to issue a credible, appropriately scoped proposal.

The call is not a sales pitch. It is a scoping conversation. The output is a clear next step — either a Resource Allocation Audit, a tailored proposal, or a referral to the most appropriate service.

---

## 2. Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes)

Before every Discovery Call, open the lead's §8 Structured Record from the intake email and confirm the following:

| Field | What to note |
|-------|-------------|
| Full Name + Role | How to address them; their authority level |
| Organisation | School, institution, or org name and any prior knowledge |
| Country / Region | Time zone confirmed; any regional considerations |
| Buyer Type | Which call framework to use (see Section 4) |
| MAOI | Primary service interest — frame the conversation around this |
| Alignment | Primary / Secondary / Context-Dependent — calibrate depth accordingly |
| Pricing Band | Know the range going in; be ready to contextualise it |
| Organisation Size | If provided — affects licence tier and group rate |
| Timeline | If provided — affects proposal urgency |
| Message | Any free-text notes from the form — read carefully before the call |
| Preferred Contact | Email / phone / WhatsApp — match their preference |

**Also check:**
- Has the lead accessed the pricing briefing? (Access link opened?)
- Is this their first contact, or did they follow up after the access link?
- Are there any existing CRM notes from previous contact?

## 3. Standard 30-Minute Call Agenda

| Time | Section | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| 0:00 – 2:00 | Welcome + context | Introduce JE/WWL briefly; confirm call purpose |
| 2:00 – 8:00 | Their situation | Open-ended: what's the context, what prompted the enquiry |
| 8:00 – 16:00 | Scoping questions | Buyer-type-specific questions (see Section 4) |
| 16:00 – 22:00 | Service alignment | Briefly describe the matched service; check fit |
| 22:00 – 27:00 | Next step | Agree what happens after this call |
| 27:00 – 30:00 | Close | Confirm follow-up action, timeline, contact |

**Call tone notes:**
- Lead with curiosity, not a pitch deck.
- Use the lead's language about their school/org, not internal JE terminology.
- Do not quote specific prices on the call unless the lead has already seen the access briefing. Frame pricing as a range subject to scoping.
- If the conversation reveals a different primary MAOI than the form indicated, note it and adjust.

## 4. Scoping Questions by Buyer Type

### School Administrator (`school_admin`)

Core questions:

1. "Can you tell me a bit about the school — age range, number of students, and the curriculum framework you're working within?"
2. "What's the current English provision, and what are you most looking to change or improve?"
3. "Are you thinking about a whole-school rollout, or a specific year group or department?"
4. "Who else is involved in this decision — is it primarily you, or does it involve a head of department, board, or finance team?"
5. "What's your rough timeline — are you planning for this academic year, or the next?"
6. "Have you thought about the teacher-training dimension, or is this primarily about the curriculum and licence?"

**Resource Allocation Audit trigger**: After this call, if school size and MAOI are confirmed, offer the no-cost Resource Allocation Audit before issuing the proposal.

---

### Teacher / CPD Lead (`teacher_cpd_lead`)

Core questions:

1. "Are you looking at this primarily for your own professional development, or as a CPD programme for your whole department or staff?"
2. "How many teachers would be involved if you were to run a group training?"
3. "Is there an existing CPD framework or accreditation requirement that this would need to align to — such as iPGCE, PGCE, or an internal performance framework?"
4. "Are you thinking about a one-off training day, or building toward a sustained pathway — for example, the Train-the-Trainer certification?"
5. "Does your school / institution already have a Jurassic English licence, or is this an independent enquiry?"
6. "What's the decision-making process for approving CPD spend at your school?"

**Note**: If the school is not yet licensed, a school_admin referral may be more valuable than a standalone CPD proposal.

---

### Institutional Partner (`institutional_partner`)

Core questions:

1. "Can you tell me about your organisation and what you do — and what you see as the potential point of overlap with JE?"
2. "Are you thinking about distribution, co-delivery, licensing, or something more bespoke?"
3. "What's the geographic scope you have in mind — regional, national, or international?"
4. "How many schools or institutions would be in your network, approximately?"
5. "Who would be the primary contact on your side for the scoping and agreement process?"
6. "Is there an existing timeline or business case driving this — for example, a funding cycle, grant period, or board decision?"

**Note**: Partnership conversations often require a follow-up Scoping Consultation (separate to the initial Discovery Call). Agree this on the call.

---

### Parent / Guardian (`parent_guardian`)

Core questions:

1. "Is your child currently at a school that uses Jurassic English, or is this an independent enquiry?"
2. "What age and year group is your child in?"
3. "What's the primary goal — supplementary support at home, or are you looking at whether the school should adopt JE?"

**Routing after this call:**
- School is already licensed → direct parent to school's JE coordinator
- School is not licensed → offer school referral pathway; provide independent DRE access info
- Parent is actually a school decision-maker exploring for their child's school → re-classify as `school_admin` and restart with school_admin questions

---

### Other (`other`)

Core questions (to reclassify first):

1. "Thanks for getting in touch — before we get into the specifics, it would help to understand a bit more about your context. What's your role, and what does your organisation do?"
2. "What prompted you to look at Jurassic English specifically?"
3. "Who would be the end users of the programme — students, teachers, or both?"

**After the first 3 questions:**
- Reclassify as the appropriate buyer type
- Continue with the matched buyer-type questions above
- Note the reclassification in the tracker

## 5. Resource Allocation Audit (RAA)

The RAA is a no-cost, post-Discovery-Call step offered to `school_admin` and `institutional_partner` leads before a formal proposal is issued.

**Purpose**: Establish the exact scope of an engagement — year groups, student numbers, teacher count, current resources, CEFR baseline, curriculum framework — so the proposal is accurate and credible.

**Format**: 45–60 minute structured call or async document exchange.

**Triggers**: Offer the RAA when:
- School size is 150+ students, or
- Multiple services are under consideration (e.g. licensing + teacher training), or
- Curriculum Review or Academic Consulting is the primary MAOI

**Do not require the RAA for**:
- Small single-module teacher training enquiries
- Parent / Guardian leads
- Simple DRE-only enquiries

**RAA output**: A short internal scope document used to populate the proposal. Not shared with the lead unless agreed.

## 6. Post-Call Actions

Complete these within 24 hours of the Discovery Call:

```
[ ] Update lead status in tracker:
    - Fit confirmed → Qualified
    - Fit unclear / rescheduled / no-show → On Hold
[ ] Log call notes in tracker Notes column (date, key findings, agreed next step)
[ ] Update CRM record with any new fields (org size, timeline, decision authority, etc.)
[ ] If Qualified:
    [ ] Offer / schedule Resource Allocation Audit (if applicable)
    [ ] Set proposal target date
    [ ] Send brief follow-up email confirming agreed next step
[ ] If On Hold:
    [ ] Log reason
    [ ] Set re-engagement date (30 days default; shorter if specific trigger named)
```

## 7. Call No-Show Protocol

If a lead does not appear for a scheduled Discovery Call:

| Attempt | Timing | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| 1st | Day of no-show | Brief email: "Missed you today — happy to reschedule" |
| 2nd | Day 3 | Second reschedule offer |
| 3rd | Day 10 | Final note: "Leaving the door open if the timing works better later" |
| After 3 attempts | Day 10+ | Move to `On Hold`; set 30-day re-engagement check |

Do not move to `Closed Lost` after a no-show unless the lead explicitly declines.

---

## 8. Qualification Checklist (end of call)

Before closing the call, confirm you can answer yes to all of these:

```
[ ] Org type and size are confirmed
[ ] Primary MAOI / service area is confirmed and understood by the lead
[ ] Decision authority is confirmed (or a path to it is established)
[ ] Timeline is realistic (current or next academic year)
[ ] Pricing range has been acknowledged (lead has seen the briefing or it was discussed)
[ ] Agreed next step is clear and has a specific date
```

If all six are yes → `Qualified`. Issue proposal within 5 business days.

If one or more are no → remain at `Awaiting Discovery Call` or move to `On Hold` with the specific blocker noted.
