Context Summary
The institution identified a gap between the volume of English instruction and the quality of student reasoning and written expression. The pilot was activated to evaluate whether Jurassic English could address this gap within existing timetable constraints, without replacing the core phonics programme.
Headline Finding
The pilot produced strong reasoning evidence and adequate teacher fidelity across the 7-week window. Operational load was within expected bounds. The institution is recommended to proceed to a full Year Group rollout in the next academic term, contingent on expanded teacher orientation for the additional 3 teachers not included in this pilot.

Jurassic English™ Pilot Executive Summary · Sample
Implementation Observations What helped delivery · what created friction
What Supported Delivery
  • Teacher Champion's pre-existing familiarity with literature-based discussion reduced orientation time by approximately 30 minutes.
  • Class size (12 per group) was well-suited to the discussion protocols — larger groups may require adapted seating logistics.
  • Weekly review meetings maintained momentum and gave teachers a structured feedback loop throughout implementation.
  • School leadership visible buy-in reduced teacher hesitation during early weeks when methodology was unfamiliar.
What Created Friction
  • Second teacher required additional support with questioning routines in Weeks 2–3; addressed via brief coaching note before Week 4 session.
  • Print materials required advance preparation — last-minute printing in Week 1 caused a minor lesson delay.
  • One scheduling disruption (school assembly) displaced a pilot session; rescheduled successfully but created timetable pressure.
  • Parent communication about the programme was informal only; a brief written note earlier would have pre-empted the parent enquiry received in Week 5.

Rationale: The pilot produced sufficient evidence across all six KPI dimensions to support a Year Group rollout recommendation. Student reasoning evidence was strong; progression fit was confirmed; operational load was within bounds. Teacher fidelity was adequate and improving. The one identified gap — teacher preparation for the expanded cohort — is manageable through a standard orientation session before the next term begins and does not require a pilot extension.

Conditions for Rollout: (1) Expanded teacher orientation for 3 additional teachers before term start. (2) Advance materials preparation of at least 5 business days before first lesson. (3) Parent communication note distributed in the first week of the new term. (4) Pilot Sponsor to confirm commercial pathway and licensing scope within 30 days.
