# Jurassic English — Pilot Pack: Content Outline and Delivery Structure
**Internal use only · Jurassic English / World Wise Learning · April 2026**

---

## Overview

The Pilot Pack is the primary gated asset delivered to institutional enquirers who request access through `/pilot-programme` → `/get-started`. It is a manually curated document set, not a single file. This document defines the content scope, delivery priority, and minimum viable structure for each asset in the pack.

Each asset can be delivered individually on request or bundled as the Full Pilot Overview Pack.

---

## Asset 1 — Full Pilot Overview Pack
**`accessRequest: pilot_overview_pack`**
**Priority: P1 — required before any consultation**

This is the first document an institutional enquirer receives. It should be concise, professional, and decision-oriented. It is not a brochure.

### Required Sections

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | What the Pilot Is | Define scope and intent in plain language. Not a sample lesson. Not a free trial. A governed evaluation with defined outputs. |
| 2 | Who It Is For | School leaders, academic directors, centre owners. Qualifying criteria: past general interest, named decision-maker, plausible timeline. |
| 3 | The 4-Phase Structure | Setup → Orientation → Guided Implementation → Review and Recommendation. Each phase: duration, activities, decision gate, outputs. |
| 4 | What Gets Measured | Student reasoning evidence, teacher fidelity, progression fit, operational load, commercial readiness. |
| 5 | What Institutions Receive | 8 concrete deliverables from scope note to final report. |
| 6 | Governance Roles | Pilot Sponsor, Academic Lead, Implementation Lead, Teacher Champion. |
| 7 | Commercial Pathways | Advisory-First, Institutional MVP, Scale Readiness. Match to buyer stage. |
| 8 | How to Get Started | One clear next step: book the scoping conversation. |

### Format
- PDF, 6–10 pages
- Branded but not designed for public distribution
- Version-controlled (include version date in footer)
- Can be sent as email attachment or Notion page link

### Content Status
- Source content: fully defined in `src/i18n/content/pilotProgramme.ts`
- Needs: layout/design pass, PDF export

## Asset 2 — Implementation Scope Overview
**`accessRequest: implementation_scope_overview`**
**Priority: P2 — send before or alongside readiness checklist**

Helps an institution understand what they need to commit before the pilot can begin. Designed for the Implementation Lead and Academic Lead.

### Required Sections

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | What the Institution Provides | Named roles (Sponsor, Academic Lead, Implementation Lead, Teacher Champion), protected timetable window, access to selected learner group, materials access agreement. |
| 2 | What Jurassic English Provides | Teacher orientation, delivery protocol, evidence capture framework, review cadence, final report. |
| 3 | Scope Variables | Minimum and maximum cohort size, class level options, programme segment choices, timeline constraints. |
| 4 | Resource Commitment | Estimated teacher prep time per week, administrator time per week, materials cost if applicable. |
| 5 | Risk Factors | What causes a pilot to produce unreliable evidence: incomplete teacher team, protected window broken, insufficient learner group, no named sponsor. |
| 6 | Pre-Launch Checklist Preview | Summary of the full readiness checklist (link to Asset 4 for detail). |

### Format
- PDF or Google Doc, 3–5 pages
- Can be shared as a working document for the institution to annotate

## Asset 3 — Reporting Sample / Executive Summary Sample
**`accessRequest: reporting_sample`**
**Priority: P2 — typically requested by senior buyers / board-facing stakeholders**

Shows institutional buyers what they will receive at the end of the pilot. Designed for Pilot Sponsors, owners, or board representatives who need to understand the evidence format before committing.

### Required Sections

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | Executive Summary (sample) | Anonymised institution context, pilot duration, learner group, implementation scope, evidence quality summary, headline recommendation. |
| 2 | KPI Block (sample) | Scored or rated view across: student reasoning evidence, teacher fidelity, progression fit, operational load, stakeholder confidence, commercial readiness. Show format; dummy values acceptable. |
| 3 | Implementation Observations (sample) | 3–4 bullet observations per category (what helped, what hindered). Show format, not real institution data. |
| 4 | Rollout Recommendation (sample) | One of five pathways: proceed to rollout, extend pilot, prepare teachers first, adjust level placement, or no rollout — with brief rationale. |
| 5 | How to Read This Report | Framing note: this document is for institutional use, not public release. Evidence cited here is structured around the agreed pilot measures, not impressionistic. |

### Format
- PDF, 4–6 pages
- Clearly marked SAMPLE or ANONYMISED ILLUSTRATION
- Include a one-line note that real reports are institution-specific

## Asset 4 — Pilot Readiness Checklist
**`accessRequest: readiness_checklist`**
**Priority: P1 — can be sent before full pack as a qualifying pre-step**

Helps institutions self-assess before committing to a pilot conversation. Also used internally to qualify whether a lead is ready for a pilot scope call.

### Checklist Structure

**Section A — Institutional Alignment (6 items)**
- [ ] A named senior sponsor can protect the pilot mandate
- [ ] There is a defined curriculum problem or adoption decision being evaluated
- [ ] A specific learner group (class, year group, or programme segment) can be dedicated to the pilot
- [ ] The pilot timeline does not conflict with exam periods, holidays, or major institutional events
- [ ] Leadership has approved investigation of a new curriculum pathway
- [ ] Parent or stakeholder communication is manageable within the pilot window

**Section B — Academic Readiness (5 items)**
- [ ] An academic lead is named and has authority over curriculum decisions
- [ ] The target CEFR level (Pre-A1 through C1) is understood or can be identified
- [ ] Teachers involved can be protected from competing CPD commitments during the pilot
- [ ] Baseline student data (level placement, recent assessment) is available
- [ ] The institution has reviewed or is open to reviewing the Jurassic Thinking Cycle methodology

**Section C — Teacher Readiness (4 items)**
- [ ] At least one Teacher Champion is identified and willing
- [ ] The teacher team can commit to a 90-minute orientation session before pilot launch
- [ ] Teachers are comfortable submitting lesson observations and fidelity notes
- [ ] There is no contractual or union constraint on adopting new curriculum materials

**Section D — Operational Capacity (5 items)**
- [ ] An Implementation Lead is named with authority over timetabling and materials
- [ ] Materials can be sourced or printed within the pilot budget
- [ ] Weekly review meetings (30–45 minutes) can be protected across the 6–8 week window
- [ ] Student work can be collected and held securely for review
- [ ] The institution can commit to a final debrief meeting with a Jurassic English representative

**Scoring Guide (for internal use)**
- 18–20 checked: High readiness — proceed to scope call
- 13–17 checked: Moderate readiness — identify gaps before committing
- Below 13: Pre-pilot advisory recommended before full pilot activation

### Format
- PDF or fillable Google Form / Notion form
- 1 page if PDF; can be a shared form if digital

## Asset 5 — Detailed Institutional Programme Pack
**`accessRequest: institutional_programme_pack`**
**Priority: P3 — for advanced buyers evaluating curriculum depth before procurement**

The full programme reference document. For school leaders, academic directors, or procurement teams who need to evaluate the curriculum architecture in depth before pilot or licensing decisions.

### Required Sections

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | Programme Architecture | Five levels (Pre-A1 through C1), age range coverage (4–14+), vertical progression logic, and how levels connect. |
| 2 | The Jurassic Thinking Cycle | Core methodology: the 5 thinking cycle stages, how they operate in a lesson, what teachers manage, what students produce. |
| 3 | Literature Selection Model | How texts are chosen, what makes a text pedagogically appropriate, thematic coherence across levels. |
| 4 | CEFR Alignment | How programme levels map to CEFR descriptors across receptive and productive skills. Reference to full CEFR alignment documentation. |
| 5 | Standards Compatibility | How the programme fits with national or regional curriculum standards (Vietnam MOET context; international school frameworks). |
| 6 | Assessment Model | How student reasoning evidence is collected, what forms of written and spoken output are assessed, how progression is tracked. |
| 7 | Teacher Role and Training | What teachers need to know, what orientation covers, what ongoing support looks like, what CPD pathway exists. |
| 8 | Institutional Use Cases | School licensing, teacher training cohorts, language academy licensing, curriculum review consulting, partner network pathways. |
| 9 | Technical and Operational Requirements | Materials format, class size guidance, room setup, technology requirements (none beyond printing). |
| 10 | Pricing and Licensing Context | Pointer to private Plans & Pricing briefing (not included in this document). |

### Format
- PDF, 12–20 pages
- Designed for institutional stakeholder review, board presentation, or procurement folder
- Can be shared as a read-only Notion workspace with section anchors
